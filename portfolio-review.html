Portfolio Review

A complimentary review can show whether the current portfolio is working hard for the right reasons.

Many families already have investments in place, but they are unsure whether the structure reflects their goals, risk capacity, liquidity needs, or future responsibilities. A focused review can bring immediate clarity.

What The Review Looks At
  • Asset allocation and concentration
  • Risk suitability and behavioural fit
  • Liquidity readiness and near-term obligations
  • Goal alignment and structural clarity
Why Reviews Matter

A second opinion often reveals issues that are invisible when the portfolio is viewed product by product.

Families frequently discover duplication, hidden concentration, unsuitable risk, or unnecessary complexity only after the portfolio is reviewed as one integrated structure.

Structure

See the whole portfolio, not isolated holdings.

A review helps move beyond products toward a clearer understanding of how the full structure actually behaves.

Suitability

Check whether the current risk really fits.

Many investors discover their portfolio is more aggressive, more illiquid, or more concentrated than they originally intended.

Direction

Identify the next best move without unnecessary complexity.

The objective is clearer decision-making: what to simplify, what to revisit, and what to align more directly with family goals.

What We Typically Review

The conversation focuses on the practical concerns serious families actually care about.

The point is not to overwhelm the prospect. It is to identify what deserves attention, why it matters, and what the next level of planning should cover.

Allocation

How capital is spread across asset classes

We assess whether the mix of growth, stability, liquidity, and resilience matches the actual job of the capital.

Overlap

Where the portfolio may be redundant or cluttered

Multiple products often create repeated exposures without improving the portfolio's real structure.

Liquidity

Whether the family can access capital when needed

Life events, retirement needs, contingencies, and peace of mind all depend on the right kind of liquidity being available.

Goals

Whether the portfolio still serves what matters most

The strongest portfolios are easier to explain because they are directly connected to real goals and responsibilities.

Especially Useful For
  • Families with multiple funds, PMS, or wealth products.
  • Executives with concentrated exposures or ESOP-related wealth.
  • Retirees or pre-retirees questioning current risk levels.
  • NRI families trying to organise scattered holdings.
How The Meeting Works

A good review meeting should feel practical, thoughtful, and immediately useful.

The aim is not to produce noise. It is to give the prospect a clearer view of what the portfolio is doing well, what may be misaligned, and what a stronger planning path would look like.

01

Understand the family's current priorities

The review starts with context: what the money is meant to do, what the near-term concerns are, and what the family wants from the conversation.

02

Read the portfolio at the structure level

Holdings are assessed together for allocation, concentration, duplication, liquidity, and suitability instead of being judged in isolation.

03

Surface what deserves immediate attention

The prospect leaves with a clearer sense of what is working, what is not, and which questions should guide the next stage.
